Lord Shiva: The Auspicious One

Origin and Significance:

Lord Shiva, also known as Mahadeva or the Great God, is a central figure in Hinduism. He represents both destruction and regeneration, symbolizing the cyclical nature of existence.

Attributes and Symbolism:

  • Third Eye (Trinetra): Lord Shiva's third eye represents wisdom, intuition, and inner vision.
  • Blue Throat (Nilakantha): His blue throat resulted from consuming the poison during the churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan).
  • Snake (Naga): Shiva wears a snake around his neck, symbolizing control over desire and ego.
  • Drum (Damaru): The rhythmic sound of Shiva's drum signifies creation and dissolution.
  • Deer Skin (Tiger Skin): His attire made of deer skin or tiger skin represents asceticism and detachment.
