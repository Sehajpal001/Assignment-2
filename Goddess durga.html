Goddess Durga

Introduction

Durga is a major Hindu goddess, worshipped as a principal aspect of the mother goddess Mahadevi. She is associated with protection, strength, motherhood, destruction, and wars. Durga is revered for her role in combating evils and demonic forces that threaten peace, prosperity, and dharma.

Attributes and Symbols

Durga is often depicted with the following attributes:

  • Weapons: Each of her multiple arms holds a weapon, symbolizing her ability to protect and fight evil.
  • Lion or Tiger: She is often shown riding a lion or tiger, representing her power and fearlessness.
  • Trident (Trishula): Symbolizes the three qualities of nature (satva, rajas, and tamas).
  • Lotus: Represents purity and detachment.

Birth and Mythology

According to legend, Durga was created by the collective energy of the gods to defeat the buffalo demon Mahishasura, who could not be defeated by any man or god. She embodies their combined strength and power.

Role in Hindu Scriptures

  • Devi Mahatmya: Describes her victory over Mahishasura and other demons.
  • Devi Bhagavata Purana: Reveres her as the primordial creator of the universe.

Worship and Festivals

Durga is worshipped by millions of Hindus around the world. Major festivals dedicated to her include:

  • Durga Puja: Celebrated with great fervor, especially in Bengal, marking her victory over Mahishasura.
  • Navaratri: A nine-night festival celebrating her various forms and powers.
  • Vijayadashami: Marks the end of Durga Puja and symbolizes the victory of good over evil.

Teachings and Significance

Durga's teachings emphasize the importance of strength, protection, and righteousness. She is considered a role model for those seeking to overcome obstacles and fight against evil.

  • Strength: Durga represents the power and resilience needed to overcome challenges.
  • Protection: She is the protector of the righteous and the destroyer of evil.
  • Righteousness: Her actions symbolize the triumph of good over evil and the importance of dharma (righteousness).
