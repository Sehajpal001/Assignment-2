Lord Krishna

Early Life

Birth

Lord Krishna was born in Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva. His birth is celebrated as Janmashtami.

Prophecy

A prophecy foretold that Devaki's eighth son would kill the tyrant king Kansa, leading to Krishna's birth in secrecy.

Escape to Gokul

To protect him from Kansa, Krishna was taken to Gokul, where he was raised by Yashoda and Nanda.

Youth

Childhood Adventures

Krishna's childhood was filled with playful and miraculous events, such as stealing butter and playing with his friends.

Defeating Demons

Krishna defeated many demons sent by Kansa, including Putana, Trinavarta, and Bakasura.

Govardhan Hill

Krishna lifted the Govardhan Hill to protect the villagers of Vrindavan from torrential rains sent by Indra.

Teachings

Bhagavad Gita

Krishna imparted the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, covering topics like duty, righteousness, and devotion.

Karma Yoga

Krishna emphasized the importance of selfless action and performing one's duty without attachment to the results.

Bhakti Yoga

He also taught the path of devotion, encouraging individuals to surrender to God and cultivate a loving relationship with the divine.

Legacy

Influence on Culture

Krishna's life and teachings have had a profound impact on Indian culture, art, music, and literature.

Festivals

Festivals like Janmashtami and Holi celebrate different aspects of Krishna's life and are widely observed by devotees.

Global Influence

Krishna's teachings have transcended cultural boundaries and continue to inspire people around the world.

Miracles

Butter Thief

Krishna's playful act of stealing butter endeared him to the villagers and showcased his divine nature.

Kaliya Mardan

Krishna subdued the serpent Kaliya, who was poisoning the Yamuna River, and danced on his hoods.

Rasa Lila

Krishna's divine dance with the gopis (cowherd girls) of Vrindavan is celebrated as Rasa Lila, symbolizing the union of the soul with the divine.

Festivals

Janmashtami

Janmashtami celebrates Krishna's birth with fasting, singing, and reenactments of his childhood stories.

Holi

Holi, the festival of colors, commemorates Krishna's playful nature and his love for Radha and the gopis.

Rasa Lila

Rasa Lila festivals reenact Krishna's divine dance with the gopis, celebrating the joy and love of the divine.
