Anjani Putra - Hanuman Ji

Birth and Early Life

Hanuman was born to Anjana, a monkey, and Vayu, the god of the wind.

He was born with incredible strength and agility, and was known for his mischievous and playful nature.

Meeting Rama and Joining the Quest

Hanuman met Rama and his brother Lakshmana while they were in exile in the forest.

He became a devoted follower of Rama and joined him on his quest to rescue Sita from the demon king Ravana.

Hanuman's incredible strength and agility made him a valuable ally in the battle against Ravana.

The Journey to Lanka

Hanuman was tasked with finding Sita and gathering information about Ravana's army.

He traveled to Lanka, Ravana's kingdom, and met with Sita, who was being held captive.

Hanuman's bravery and cunning allowed him to evade Ravana's guards and return to Rama with vital information.

The Battle of Lanka

Hanuman played a crucial role in the battle against Ravana, using his strength and agility to fight off the demon king's army.

He was instrumental in the defeat of Ravana and the rescue of Sita.

The battle was a testament to Hanuman's bravery and devotion to Rama.

Worship and Legacy

Hanuman is worshipped by millions of Hindus around the world, and is considered a symbol of strength, courage, and devotion.

His temples can be found all over India and other parts of the world, and his image is often depicted in art and literature.

Hanuman's legacy continues to inspire and guide people around the world, and his story is retold and reinterpreted in many different ways.

Teachings and Significance

Hanuman's teachings emphasize the importance of devotion, loyalty, and self-discipline.

He is considered a role model for Hindus, and his story is often told to children to teach them about the importance of bravery, loyalty, and duty.

Hanuman's significance extends beyond Hinduism, and his story has been retold and reinterpreted in many different cultures and traditions.
