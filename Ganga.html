Goddess Ganga

Introduction

Ganga is the Hindu goddess who personifies the Ganges River. She is worshipped as the goddess of purification and forgiveness. Ganga is often depicted as a beautiful woman riding a divine crocodile-like creature called the makara.

Attributes and Symbols

Ganga is often depicted with the following attributes:

  • Makara: A mythical creature, part crocodile and part dolphin, symbolizing her dominion over water.
  • Kalasha: A water pot, representing purity and life.
  • White Lotus: Denotes purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Birth and Mythology

Ganga is believed to have descended from the heavens to purify the earth. She is considered the daughter of Himavan, the personification of the Himalayas, and the sister of the goddess Parvati. Legends focus on her descent to earth, which was facilitated by the god Shiva.

Role in Hindu Scriptures

  • Rigveda: Ganga is mentioned as the holiest of rivers.
  • Puranas: She is described as the purifier of sins and the mother of the warrior Bhishma.

Worship and Festivals

Ganga is worshipped by millions of Hindus. Major festivals dedicated to her include:

  • Ganga Dussehra: Celebrates her descent to earth.
  • Ganga Jayanti: Marks her birth.

Teachings and Significance

Ganga's teachings emphasize the importance of purification and forgiveness. She is considered a motherly figure who nurtures and purifies humanity.

  • Purification: Bathing in the Ganges is believed to cleanse one of all sins.
  • Forgiveness: Ganga is the embodiment of forgiveness and mercy.
