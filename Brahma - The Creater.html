Brahma- The creator.

A prominent figure in Hindu mythology, Brahma is regarded as the universe's creator.

Origin and Creation:

  • The universe began when Brahma emerged from Hiranyagarbha, a golden cosmic egg.
  • Brahma, the creator god of the Vedas, is credited with creating the earth and everything on it.

    • Role and Attributes:

  • Lord Vishnu, the preserver, and Lord Shiva, the destroyer, form the Hindu trinity known as the Trimurti, which also includes Brahma.
  • He is linked to the Vedas, creation, and wisdom. (ancient Hindu scriptures).
  • Typically portrayed with four arms bearing an alms bowl, a bow, prayer beads, and a book, Brahma has four faces, signifying his infinite potential.
  • He rides a goose, and the goddesses Sarasvati and Savitri frequently accompany him.

    • Decline in Worship:

  • The Trimurti remained important, but Brahma's status as the ultimate deity declined by the seventh century.
  • Though there aren't many temples dedicated solely to Brahma these days, you can still find his image in temples honoring Shiva or Vishnu.
